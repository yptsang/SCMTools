Temporal risk is categorized using a Pareto-style cumulative percentage based on the frozen period length (in days).

  • High: Items accounting for the top 80% of total frozen period days. Highest temporal risk.
  • Medium: Items accounting for the next 15% (up to 95%). Moderate temporal risk.
  • Low: The remaining items (bottom 5% of frozen days). Lowest temporal risk.

Demand variability is categorized based on the Coefficient of Variation (CV = Standard Deviation / Mean).

  • Class X: Very stable demand (CV ≤ 0.5). High forecast accuracy possible.
  • Class Y: Moderate variability (0.5 < CV ≤ 1.0). Safety stock needed.
  • Class Z: High variability (CV > 1.0). Difficult to forecast.
